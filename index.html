Experiences & Projects

Babbelbord

Babbelbord is a boardgame that our team at the University of Twente designed to enhance the reminiscence therapy for people affected by dementia. Through a gamified and more engaging experience, this prototype aims to facilitate the conversation between patients and family/caregivers. One interesting aspect consist in the personalized game experience: when a patient prefers to avoid answering to specific questions, these get added to their profile and skipped in the future games.
Hardware-wise, an Arduino Mega microcontroller is embedded in the board, and it senses where the 3D printed pawns equipped with magnets have been placed. This information then get sent to the NodeJS server, which through a series of custom APIs allows the communication with a PostgreSQL database. The questions pop up on a tablet, where we load the web application developed in VueJS.

I contributed by implementing the whole softare part of the server, client and database.

Beathoven

With Beathoven, we researched new ways to allow deaf people to experience music. To achieve this goal, we tried to combine different sensory modalities such as haptics and sight. We therefore implemented a vibrating forearm glove, augmenting the experience with VR.

I contributed by implementing the connection to Spotify APIs, and coding some parts using Processing and Unity.

JustaCar

For the Design project course in Aalto University, we were asked to design new services regarding mobility-as-a-services (MAAS). After our team went through the divergent phase of design and explored several opportunities, we eventually focused on Justacar.
Justacar is a distributed car rental service that offers only low-end vehicles (the insight of focusing on these vehicles came from the user research). We then created and tested a prototype, making sure that the business would also be profitable.

LivLab Spese Coop

During my internship in Telecom Italia JOL SKIL innovation centre, I have worked on the design of a mobile app for one of the largest retailers in Italy.
Initially, I did some literature research to have a better understanding of the domain. Subsequently, thanks to the living lab organized by COOP, we could gather insightful qualitative research from a pool of more than 100 customers. This gave us a better understanding of their motivations and frustrations, and we could use this data to propose a solution that followed a human-centred design approach.

My contribution consisted in creating personas, scenarios and customer journey map from the insights we gathered qualitative and quantitative research.

Features extracted from the data to create personas.

Rapida Mango

After conducting some research, our team discovered a design opportunity: the food management system of the hospital in Trento was still carried out on paper, therefore giving us room for improvement to such service. We conducted interviews with the hospital staff to get acquainted with the problem domain, subsequently designing a service that takes into account all the actors in the network, from kitchen staff to patients.

Screen that allows nurses to know which patients need help to order food.

meSch project

For my graduation thesis I worked on meSch, a EU funded project to enhance the museum/heritage site experience of the visitors by allowing professional heritage curators to use a common platform to create new experiences.

My contribution to the project consisted in designing the interfaces that the curators are going to use to create sophisticated "recipes" of interactive experiences, giving also the chance to other professionals to re-use their ideas and thus creating a community of experts. To pursue this goal I created both the paper and the digital prototypes, subsequently testing them in different evaluation sessions and iteratively redesigning with the feedback received.
